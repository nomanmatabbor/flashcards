Amperometry 	titrimetric method, used to calculate equivalence point, through current measurement produced by reactants, at constant applied potential.
The current magnitude	depends on the concentration of the analyte.
Principal	constant potential, limiting current proportional to conc of reactant/product, according to Ilkobic equation. 
Diffusion current 	= limiting current - residual current
Amperometry is also called 	potentiometric titrations
Electrodes in amperometry	1. Polarizable, 2. Non-polarizable, 3. Indicator microelectrode: dropping mercury electrode (DME) or Pt electrode, 4. Reference electrode: SCE
